# Story: Leading a team through a tight deadline

<!-- STAR format. Used for: "Tell me about a time you led a team under pressure" -->
<!-- tags: leadership, pressure, delivery, mentoring -->

## Situation

In Q3 2023, a compliance requirement meant we had to ship a data retention feature in 2 weeks
or face regulatory risk. The feature touched 3 different services and hadn't been scoped yet.
I was asked to take it on as tech lead.

## Task

I needed to scope, plan, and deliver the feature with a team of 4 — two of whom were mid-level
engineers who hadn't worked on the affected services before.

## Action

- I ran a 2-hour scoping session to map the affected systems and break the work into daily
  milestones, making sure we had a clear definition of done for each piece.
- I assigned each engineer to a service they could own end-to-end, and paired the two newer
  engineers with senior teammates for the first 2 days.
- I held a 15-minute standup each morning focused only on blockers, and personally unblocked
  the two juniors each afternoon rather than waiting for them to escalate.
- When we hit an unexpected data migration issue on day 9, I made the call to simplify the
  migration logic at the cost of a manual backfill step, which kept us on schedule.

## Result

Delivered on time, zero post-release defects over the first 2 weeks. The CTO recognized the
team in the all-hands meeting. Both junior engineers said it was the most they'd learned
in a single sprint.
